- **DOUG????** addressed the members and spoke regarding his preference
to return to general meeting each month as he felt members were not
'kept in the loop' at Social Forums/Meetings. Jamie Tanner responded
that members can be 'kept in the loop' at Social Forums but matters
obviously cannot be passed at Social Forums.

Graeme Gaunsen agreed with Doug, saying members are not
being informed and that the sub branch should stay with tradition of
general meetings each month.

Murray Warlick asked if the sub branch went back to monthly
meetings would a meal be provided.

The members were asked if there were any members against this
proposal

**Janice Peate** addressed the members saying it was her belief that
keeping members/people informed was difficult with so many voluntary
positions being filled sporadically due to time/work restraints and
that unless more members stood up as volunteers, having formal
meetings every month will cause further imposition to an already busy
executive.

**Colleen Wills** addressed the members saying she found the social
forums a good source of information, socialization and enjoyed guest
speakers.

**DOUG????** Responded that guest speakers could be invited to general
meetings and didn't have to stay for business. He said that he didn't
feel the members were receiving emails.

**Dick Wills** thanked everyone for their comments, saying the above
would be referred to the President but re-iterated that in his time as
President and Vice President fulfilling positions in the office has
not been easy. The current Secretary has a full time employment
position, there have been six administration officers over the past
few years, Aaron Ryan took ill in early January, and is still in
hospital. Dick said the the emailing system had been changed and that
without Aaron Ryan to train Toni Cromack, there had been a delay in
emailing members. Dick spoke against the motion.

**Allan Britt** brought a Point of Order to move a Motion that the
matter be Taken On Notice, with notice being two weeks prior to the
next meeting. Dick Wills said there is no formal two week requirement
since the changes in SOP as of 2019.

It was decided that the President will discuss the matter at a
following meeting *"That the social meeting every second month be
returned to general meeting". **DOUGXXXX** requested it be put to a
vote. It was put to a vote with the result being not in favour.*

**Motion defeated**

## Declaration of vacancies

Jamie Tanner advised that all positions are vacant.

The Returning Officer advised the meeting that there were only three
written nominations received, one each for the positions of President,
Vice-president, and Secretary, with one of the nomination received
after the close of the nominations date and time as advised in
accordance with SOP4. He subsequently advised that there being one
nomination for each position, no ballot was required. To resolve the
late nomination matter, the members could accept the late nomination
without any one dissent, and proceed to declaring the positions
nominated for, or if dissented, then to declare two positions,
carrying three casual vacancies to the next General Meeting or
subsequent Special General meeting. No dissent was declared, the
Returning Officer declared Jamie Tanner elected President, Michael
Patch elected Vice-president, and Michael Wright elected Secretary,
and that casual vacancies exist for the positions of Vice-president
and Treasurer.

Two casual positions will be created, second Vice President and
Treasurer, to be filled at next meeting. Col Macdonald will remain as
a signatory until these positions are filled. Anyone nominating for
these positions has to have their nominations in by the May meeting.

No nominations for a Committee were required as the Executive meets
each Wednesday morning any member wishing to attend is welcome.

The incoming executive brief spoke and introduced themselves to the
members, newly appointed President James Tanner thanked the previous
executive and outgoing office administrator for their efforts during
their term of office.
